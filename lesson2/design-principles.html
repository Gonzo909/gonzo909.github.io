Visual Heirarchy

Visual hierarchy is the principle of arranging elements to show their order of importance. The title here is the biggest element on the page, and the buttons are smaller, and the text is the smallest.

PARC: Contrast

X.com

mostly used to emphasise the difference between selected elements, and is created when two elements are very different. The text on the website is clearly visible, as well as the images and icons to navigate throughout the website

PARC: Repetition

Common elements, such as colors, graphics, layout styles, and so on, should be repeated to give a consistent look and feel to a page. The sections for the products are all the same, and the colors are consistent throughout the website.
